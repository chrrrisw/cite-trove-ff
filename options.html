Cite Trove How-To

This Firefox addon allows users of the Trove Newspaper site to copy selected OCR text to their system clipboard along with information such as the Newspaper Title, Issue and Page reference.

The format of the copied text can be configured through this preferences page.

To modify the format, use the following codes along with any other text of your choice.

  • %A - Persistent Article URL (preferred)
  • %a - APA citation
  • %C - Current date and time
  • %H - Article Headline
  • %h - Harvard/Australian citation
  • %I - Newspaper Issue
  • %m - MLA citation
  • %n - Start a new line
  • %P - Article Page number
  • %p - Newspaper Page URL
  • %Q - Selected OCR Text
  • %T - Newspaper Title
  • %U - Article URL (see note below)
  • %u - Shortened Article URL (without search parameters etc, see note below)
  • %w - Wikipedia citation

NOTE: The URLs given by both %U and %u are NOT the preferred way to link to an article - they may change (and have changed in the past) with updates to the Trove website. The URL given by %A is guaranteed by the NLA to be the persistent into the future, and is therefore to be preferred over the others.

Example 1:

%T, %I, page %P

Would produce the something like:

The Canberra Times (ACT : 1926 - 1995), Thursday 3 July 1975, page 6

Example 2:

Title: %T%nIssue: %I, page %P%n[Quote start]%n%Q%n[Quote end]%n

Would produce the something like:

Title: The Canberra Times (ACT : 1926 - 1995)
Issue: Thursday 3 July 1975, page 6
[Quote start]
Space
MOSCOW, Wednesday (AAP). - The Soviet and US space agencies wound up preparations yesterday for this month's Soyuz-Apollo link-up in an atmosphere of "deep satisfaction and mutual understanding", according to Tass news agency.
[Quote end]

Original format:

The original format is: %A%n%C%n%T%n%I, page %P%n[Quote]%n%Q%n[Quote]%n

Which produces something like:

http://nla.gov.au/nla.news-article110644884
2013-06-11T01:10:55.553Z
The Canberra Times (ACT : 1926 - 1995)
Thursday 3 July 1975, page 6
[Quote]
Space
MOSCOW, Wednesday (AAP). - The Soviet and US space agencies wound up preparations yesterday for this month's Soyuz-Apollo link-up in an atmosphere of "deep satisfaction and mutual understanding", according to Tass news agency.
[Quote]
